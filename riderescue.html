Product Design

RideRescue

Challenge + Opportunity

RideRescue is a mobile app concept I developed as part of an online course on UX design. The goal of the course was to create an experience that improves upon a current product or service. I decided to address the difficulties in the car rental industry through a mobile app designed to help people in emergencies. My initial research reflected that many people who rent vehicles do so out of immediate necessity, particularly when they have just been in an accident. I sought to create a service that would unify a user’s rental options across companies and locations into one seamless experience, giving the user maximum control over their turbulent circumstances. Users could order basic, smaller vehicles to be delivered on-demand or coordinate with a nearby branch to arrange a pickup. Users also benefit from having a full survey of their options before making a decision.

Process + Result

  • Market Research - I conducted interviews with current rental customers to determine if this product was viable.
  • User Research - I presented potential customers with features and user flow exercises to establish the base user needs and created personas to categorize types of users.
  • Prototyping and Testing - I created a series of screen designs and presented them to my user groups and observed their interactions.
  • Iteration and Final Design - After progressing through rounds of testing, I made gradual alterations to the interfaces and user flows until I arrived at a final conceptual model and visual identity.

The final product was an application that allowed users to control a car rental with the immediacy they've come to expect from other car sharing services. RideRescue allows users to arrange a delivery or pickup, update their payment settings, elect their vehicle, and manage rental terms all without needing to set foot in a rental branch.
